Warholesque Paintings
Silkscreen ink and synthetic polymer paint on canvas

Ron English pays homage to Andy Warhol by pushing Warholian ideas to their logical next step—logical as defined by Ron English.

Employing the same materials, techniques, processes, and master printers (Donald Sheridan and Alexander Heinrici), English transforms iconic Warhol imagery into something unmistakably his own. These works include Cowgirls with exaggerated udders, a half-zipped Dandy Banana, Young Andy portraits, obese Ronald McDonalds (street name: MC Supersized), three-eyed Elvis figures, a Cowgirl Guernica diorama, and one of English’s most recognizable images: Marilyn Monroe with Mickey Mouse breasts.

Other Warhol-inspired works include Bruce Lee Action Actor, Figment (Dead Andy), Super Sunflower, and Abraham Obama (Barack Obama fused with Abraham Lincoln).

All paintings in the Warholesque theme are hand-painted in unique color combinations using synthetic polymer paint, incorporating the silkscreen-on-canvas method popularized by Warhol. No two works are identical.

Process

  1. Ron paints an original work, usually monotone and without a background.
  2. A high-resolution photograph is taken and used to produce a silkscreen.
  3. Canvases are cut and gessoed.
  4. The silkscreen is applied in light brown.
  5. Ron paints each canvas in unique color combinations, using the screened image as a guide. Once completed, the light screen layer is no longer visible.
  6. The silkscreen is applied again in black.
  7. The canvases are stretched.

The result is a unique Ron English painting executed through Warholian methods, carrying English’s own visual language and conceptual perspective.
