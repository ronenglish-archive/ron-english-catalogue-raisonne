Biography

Ron English is an American artist born on June 3, 1959, in Decatur, Illinois. Working across painting, drawing, sculpture, printmaking, public art, photography, design, and digital media, English has developed a body of work that combines technical precision with satire, cultural critique, and a deep engagement with the visual language of popular culture.

Often described as the “Godfather of Street Art,” English is closely associated with culture jamming, billboard interventions, and the term “Popaganda,” which he coined to describe his fusion of advertising, politics, art history, consumer culture, and pop imagery.

Artist Overview

English’s work frequently reimagines familiar icons, mascots, corporate symbols, and art-historical references, transforming them into layered images that are at once playful, unsettling, and critical.

Across decades of practice, he has built a visual vocabulary of recurring characters, grinning figures, altered cartoons, anatomical hybrids, and surreal pop-cultural forms. His work moves between studio painting and public intervention, between fine art and mass culture, and between humor and critique, making him one of the most recognizable figures associated with contemporary pop surrealism, street art, and culture-jamming traditions.
