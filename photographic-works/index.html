Overview

Ron English’s photographic works occupy an important place in his early practice. English has explained that he studied photography in college, staged hand-drawn figures in public settings for photographs, and later expanded that approach by altering billboard backgrounds with his own imagery, leaving the photograph as the lasting record. Published surveys of his career have likewise described his work as ranging from staged photography to painting, street art, and culture jamming. Because exact dates for many individual works on this page remain unconfirmed, they are presented together here as photographic works rather than divided into narrower chronological sections.

Sources: Popaganda, “Ron English’s Meditations on Street Art”; Popaganda: The Art and Subversion of Ron English; WIRED, “Popaganda, Ron English Style”.
