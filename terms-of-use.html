Terms of Use, Copyright & Research Notice

The Ron English Catalogue Raisonné is provided as a public research, scholarship, and reference resource. This notice explains the scope of the catalogue, limitations on reliance, copyright and image-rights restrictions, citation expectations, and policies for corrections and submissions.

Purpose of the catalogue

The Ron English Catalogue Raisonné is being developed as a long-term public research resource documenting works, projects, editions, studies, exhibitions, publications, and related materials associated with Ron English's practice. The catalogue is intended to support scholarship, reference, preservation, and accurate citation.

Catalogue entries are compiled from available studio records, exhibition materials, publications, archival documentation, verified online sources, images, correspondence, and other research materials. The site may include images, screenshots, links, and documentary references where they help identify, contextualize, or verify a work or related object.

Ongoing research and revisions

The catalogue is a continuing research project. Entries may be revised, corrected, expanded, reorganized, or removed as additional information becomes available. Dates, titles, dimensions, media, exhibition histories, literature references, ownership histories, related-object information, and other details may be updated over time.

Because the catalogue is built from both historical and current sources, the absence of a work, image, exhibition, publication, or related object from the site does not necessarily mean that it is not connected to Ron English. It may be undocumented, unreviewed, pending further research, outside the current scope of the public site, or not yet entered.

No certificate, appraisal, or warranty

Inclusion of a work or related object in the catalogue reflects the project's current research record and does not create any warranty or guarantee regarding authenticity, attribution, title, provenance, condition, ownership, value, or legal status.

Any formal authentication, certification, licensing, reproduction permission, or rights determination must be made separately in writing by the appropriate rights holder, authorized representative, or other qualified party. When citing the catalogue, please include the catalogue name, entry or page title, URL, and access date. Because entries may be revised over time, the access date is important.

Corrections and updates

Submitted information may be reviewed, retained, compared with existing records, and used to update the catalogue when appropriate. The catalogue retains discretion over whether, when, and how to revise public entries.

Corrections should include as much specific information as possible, such as the relevant entry title, URL, images, source citations, publication details, exhibition information, dates, dimensions, medium, provenance details, and any other documentation that helps evaluate the proposed revision.

Do not submit confidential, restricted, or private information unless you are authorized to do so and understand that it may be retained for research records. Separate written permission may be required before submitted images or documents are published on the public site.
